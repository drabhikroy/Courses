People who conduct social network analysis (SNA) are often tasked to describe complex methodologies applied to highly connected data and any corresponding results in layman’s terms. This is further compounded by the idea that networks are neither quantitative nor quantitative by design which makes it fairly unique. In fact, to steal a term from Physics, a given network constructed only of data is in methodological superposition, in that until a measurement is taken it is both quantitative and qualitative at the same time and we don’t have to consider if a cat is alive or dead or both. However unlike physics we can get information about both.2
