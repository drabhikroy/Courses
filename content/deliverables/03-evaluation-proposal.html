Type

Team Submission. Students in the course must participate in the writing process in a group oriented setting.

Begin Work

at the earliest right after the stakeholder interview, focus group, etc.

at the latest as soon as your team has submitted the Needs Assessment

Criteria

Much of the information from the previous task will help provide a context for the proposal, but the narrative should be written to address both (1) the need for your evaluation and (2) details on how the team intends to administer the evaluation itself. To accomplish this, keep in mind the following must be addressed in your writing

a concise description of the program

a description of stakeholder needs

at least one evaluation question that is clearly defined

a completed logic model

what needs to be accomplished

an anticipated timeline of evaluation activities

how the results will be delivered

Body

In your writing make sure to use a professional tone and to ensure it reads with one voice. This assignment usually fills seven (7) to 12 double spaced pages.

As you construct this, please use the items in bold as headings and subheadings. These topics are what you should cover, at minimum, in your proposal. Your answers should be brief, to the point, and must address the given topic area explicitly.

  • Introduction. A brief description of the program concentrating on what areas of importance you discovered in the Needs Assessment. This should be brief and is typically no more than one (1) page maximum.

  • Justification. You must include evidence from, at minimum, the items we have covered in the course so far as a defense for your approach. In this case, you are providing an evidence-based argument for the way you want to conduct the study. At minimum, the topics covered here should address an intended evaluative framework/orientation/model/approach that will guide your program evaluation and answer how this construct will be well-suited to evaluate the program.

  • Methodology. This section will be the largest and most detailed component. This is will be your chance to detail the intended evaluative process and to justify your methodological approach. At minimum, please cover the following where applicable

    • Sampling frame. A description of the overall targeted population and sample that you have drawn or will draw. This includes a description of the primary stakeholders, or those you are studying, and secondary stakeholders, or those who are not directly affected by the program.

    • Assessment of Values. Address what set of values you will be using in your evaluation. This should be very explicit, and you must explain where these came from?

    • Existing Data. If applicable, provide a description of what existing data will you be using within your evaluation, how you plan on accessing that data and what form the raw data will be in.

    • Data Collection. Discuss what data you will utilize within your evaluation, how you will collect that data, what instruments and/or materials you will be using or plan to use, and how you will gain permission from those who are participating.

    • Analysis. Provide a narrative on the type of raw data you will have or intend to collect. Address how you intend to analyze the quantitative, qualitative, and or mixed data you will have.

    • Reporting Cycle. Briefly discuss how the results will be reported to the sponsor, when you intend to do so, and if you are intending to provide an interim report.

    • Statement of Intent. On a separate page, please provide a one paragraph statement of intent on whether you plan to use any of your work beyond the framework of this course and your corresponding reasoning.
